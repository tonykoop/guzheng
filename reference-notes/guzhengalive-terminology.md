## Drawing Label Guidance

- Put Dragon/head and Phoenix/tail orientation labels on every full-body sheet.
- Label fixed bridges separately from movable bridges so bridge-corridor tests
  do not blur into end-point geometry.
- Label sound holes as review targets, not copied decorative shapes.
- Show the peg board and tuning box as load/access features before tuning-pin
  drilling.
- Mark string rests/nuts as wear surfaces that need material and edge-radius
  review.
- Keep pinyin/Chinese-character labels optional in drawings until reviewed by a
  fluent speaker or guzheng player; English functional labels are enough for
  shop validation.

## Validation Questions To Carry Forward

- Does the reviewed DXF/CAD package show clear head/tail orientation?
- Are fixed bridge, movable bridge, string rest, and string guide labels
  distinct?
- Are backboard sound holes named and treated as acoustic openings rather than
  decorative cutouts?
- Does the internal frame drawing identify peg board, sound beams/posts, and
  side-board load path?
- Are concept images prevented from supplying sound-hole shapes, bridge
  placement, or string-guide locations?
